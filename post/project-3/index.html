Bachelor's Thesis: Analysis of Content Based- and Collaborative Filtering for a Movie Recommender System

December 14, 2020

Disclaimer: This is an ongoing work, started in early October 2020 and will be delivered by the beginning of February 2021. This work is under the supervision and coordination of the University of Hamburg. Latest update : 11.12.2020

Recommender Systems or recommendation systems (sometimes replacing ‚system‘ with a synonym such as ‚platform‘ or ‚engine‘) are an evolving part of the modern industry’s toolbox, focused on customer acquisition, churn management, and revenue improvement. They are being used by most, if not all, information and technology leading companies around the world like Facebook, Amazon, Netflix, etc. Being able to define the personal tendencies of each individual and form personalized recommendations in form of advertisement individually unsupervised can be a major factor deciding the survivability, progress, and competitive advantage of every tech company, as selective exposure of prospective/current customers can influence decision making in their favor. The dataset used in this Thesis is the official 100k MovieLens Dataset in connection with the TMDB Dataset. Links to both can be found here: MovieLens 100k & TMDB Metadata

The research goals of this thesis are:

  • Research and Implementation of different Pre-Processing techniques to manipulate data through Python. There is an excessive amount of factors that could alter the behavior and learning patterns of a given dataset, from missing (or false) values, to inconvenient importing of data. The elimination of such hindrances could benefit the implementation of the main methods and result in clearer and better conclusions. The Pre-Processing includes the following steps:

    • Missing and NaN value handling
    • Feature Standardization and/or Normalization
    • Outlier Detection & Handling
    • Data Cohesion/Correlation Matrix testing
    • Exploratory Data Analysis We also will explore metrics such as data density, sparsity, and various others to have a better grasp and understanding of the data given to us.

  • Following the Pre-Processing we will implement different types of Recommender Systems and evaluate the results in order to draw conclusions on the advantages and disadvantages of each, based on different example scenarios, while focusing on deducting the ideal environment to use each. Here we see the Recommender Algorithms embedded in this Thesis and the preliminary results each has yielded so far (update: 11.12.2020, input: ‘Dark Knight, The (2008)'):

    • Simple Top - N Recommender

    • Content Based- Filtering

    • Collaborative Filtering (Memory - User Based & Item Based) –> Example from Item-Based search

    • Collaborative Filtering (Model)

  • Last but not least, we will evaluate the finding by computing various evaluation metrics such as: MAE (Mean Absolute Error), MSE (Mean Squared Error), RMSE (Root Mean Squared Error), etc.
